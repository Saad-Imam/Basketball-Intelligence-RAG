Term: 2-1-2 press defense

--- Concise Definition ---
2-1-2 press defense : Basketball defensive strategy that implements traps near the sidelines to disrupt the scoring effectiveness of the offensive team with the additional possibility of creating turnovers.

What is the 2-1-2 press defense in basketball?

The 2-1-2 press defense is a basketball strategy that implements a modest amount of on-ball pressure, particularly in the backcourt, and sideline traps, especially in the frontcourt, to disrupt the scoring effectiveness of the offensive team with the additional possibility of creating turnovers.

In reference to primary responsibilities, X1 and X2 should generally pressure the ball and influence the ball handler towards a sideline, protect the middle area of the court, or set traps.

X3 should initially cover the middle area in the backcourt or the ball side wing area in the frontcourt. Additionally, X3 could also help set traps in some instances.

As for X4 and X5, those defenders should generally set traps or protect the basket.

What is a possible advantage of the 2-1-2 press defense?

One possible advantage of the 2-1-2 press defense is that there is a defender in the middle of the press who fills the gap. This means that theoretically, it would very challenging for the offensive team to simply pass the ball into the middle of the press as that could lead toturnovers.

Furthermore, since a defender occupies the middle of the press, this means that the player with the ball, particularly in the backcourt area, is effectively forced to dribble near the sidelines, which makes it easier for the defensive team toset traps.
